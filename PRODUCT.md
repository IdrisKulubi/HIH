# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Programme staff and governance roles working inside the BIRE / Hand in Hand portal: admins, verifiers, evaluators, Access to Finance officers, and the Access to Finance Committee. They use the system in offices or hybrid meetings, often under time pressure, with legal and audit expectations on decisions (scores, GAIR, IC outcomes).

Applicants use a separate, simpler portal surface; internal dashboards are not for them.

## Product Purpose

Digitize the enterprise application lifecycle from Call for Applications through due diligence, capacity building, matching grant investment, and disbursement. Dashboards must make status, next actions, and accountability obvious so staff can move cases forward without hunting through screens.

Success looks like: fewer missed handoffs between roles, traceable committee decisions, and consistent UI across modules (admin, A2F officer, committee).

## Brand Personality

Professional, clear, accountable. Three words: trustworthy, efficient, humane. Tone is institutional but not cold: plain language, no marketing fluff on operational screens.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing aesthetics on internal tools (gradient heroes, glass cards, decorative motion).
- Left-accent stripe stat cards and oversized hero metrics.
- Crypto/fintech neon-on-black or healthcare stock teal-on-white defaults without programme context.
- Modal-first workflows where inline review would suffice.
- Inconsistent role shells (each dashboard feeling like a different product).

## Design Principles

1. **Task first:** Every screen answers what is in front of me and what I do next.
2. **Earned familiarity:** Reuse patterns (tables, filters, top nav) across roles; vary accent color by module, not layout grammar.
3. **Decision traceability:** Scores, GAIR, and committee outcomes must be readable in place, not buried.
4. **Restrained color:** Neutrals carry the UI; module accents (emerald officer, brand blue committee, brand blue/red programme) signal context only.
5. **Accessible by default:** WCAG 2.1 AA contrast, visible focus, reduced-motion respect, keyboard-friendly tables and forms.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Support keyboard navigation for tables and dialogs; do not rely on color alone for status (pair badges with text). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for non-essential animation. Use clear labels on form controls in committee decision flows.
